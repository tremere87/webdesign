1.5 Describing copyright and other constraints affecting websites

Copyright is the exclusive and assignable legal right, to reproduce the work, usually with the grounds to make a profit from the subject. This right is not absolute but is usually considered to be binding. Copyrights can be granted by public law and are in that case considered "territorial rights". This means that copyrights granted by the law of a certain state, do not extend outside of that territory influence.

For thE purpose of this work, the relevence of Copyright with creative work is significant to course grading. Whilst something like wikipedia is an open platform, that anyone can add to; the information added is considered to be factual, for the purposes of referencing it. Citations must be used for anymore than 10% of someone else's work, re-used in your own projects, must be cited as a reference, otherwise the work will be considered plagiarised and the examining body will fail the student.

A major issue that would arise with a website design is, if it needs to be accessible is those with visual impairments. Whilst it will be possible to write in the code to make a website accessible to impaired users of any type; normally the device they are accessing your site with will usually have the features to allow them to, already. This is just as well, as coding a website specifically for a disability is way beyond my coding skill.
